Pronunciation tips:

1. negotiate: (1) Four parts: ne-go-ti-ate /nəˈɡoʊ.ʃi.eɪt/ (2) The pronunciation of “e” is /ə/ not /ɪ/
2. promotion: (1) Three parts: pro-mo-tion /prəˈmoʊ.ʃən/ (2) The pronunciation of /oʊ/:
．Similar sound: snow, go, coat.
．How to pronounce: First, round your lips and make them tense. Second, pull them in a tighter circle.
3. autonomy: (1) Four parts: au-to-no-my /ɑːˈtɑː.nə.mi/ (2) In American English, the pronunciation of the first part is /ɑ/, instead of /ɔ/.

1.
* negotiate [nɪˋgoʃɪ͵et] (v.) （就…）談判
: to have formal discussions with someone in order to reach an agreement with them
- Both parties will send their delegation to negotiate the ceasefire agreement.
雙方都將派出代表團談判停戰協議。

2.
* promotion [prəˋmoʃən] (n.) 升遷
: the act of raising someone to a higher or more important position or rank
- I was replaced by the new employee, who thus got the promotion here to the headquarter.
我被新員工取代了，他因此得到了總部的晉升。

3.
* autonomy [ɔˋtɑnəmɪ] (n.) 自主權
: the ability to make your own decisions without being controlled by anyone else
- If parents interfere too much in their children's life, they will slow down children’s development in autonomy.
如果父母過多地干涉孩子的生活，他們會減緩孩子在自主性方面的發展。
