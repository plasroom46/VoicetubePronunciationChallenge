We should learn, regularly, to travel around our minds and think it almost as prestigious to sit at home and reflect on a trip we once took to an island as to trek to this island encased in our cumbersome bodies.

我們應該有規律地學習如何在腦中遨遊，並且認為在家裡坐著回想曾經經歷過小島遊這件事，其重要程度就跟我們的軀殼在腦上行走一般。

Pronunciation tips:

1. regularly (the pronunciations of the vowels here)
2. prestigious (the pronunciations of e and giouse)
3. sit at (linking sound)
4. reflect on (linking sound)
5. trek (how to pronounce tre)
6. cumbersome (the ə sounds in the word)

1.
* prestigious [prɛsˋtɪdʒɪəs] (adj.) 有威望的
: very much respected and admired, usually because of being important
- Oxford is one of the most prestigious universities in the UK.
牛津大學是英國最負盛名的大學之一。

2.
* trek [trɛk] (v.) （徒步）長途跋涉
: to walk a long distance, usually over land such as hills, mountains, or forests
- We spent the day trekking through forests and over mountains.
白天我們穿過叢林，翻山越嶺。

3.
* cumbersome [ˋkʌmbɚsəm] (adj.) 笨重的
: awkward because of being large, heavy, or not effective
- People might regard the mechanism as too cumbersome.
人們可能會認為該機製過於繁瑣。
