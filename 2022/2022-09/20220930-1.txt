Pronunciation tips:

1. judgmental: (1) Two parts: judg-mental /dʒʌdʒˈmen.t̬əl/ (2) /dʒ/ appears in the begging and the ending of the first part (3) /dʒ/: teeth closed (4) t̬: flap t
2. argumentative: (1) Five parts: ar-gu-men-ta-tive /ˌɑːrɡ.jəˈmen.t̬ə.t̬ɪv/ (2) Slight /y/ sound
3. sarcastic: (1) Three parts: sar-ca-stic /sɑːrˈkæs.tɪk/ (2) Notice: /æ/ sound. The tongue is closed to the bottom of the mouth and the tip of the tongue is at the front of the mouth.

1.
* judgmental [dʒʌdʒˋmɛnt!] (adj.) 動不動就批評人的
: too quick to criticize people
- She is judgmental about everyone except for herself.
她只會批評別人。

2.
* argumentative [͵ɑrgjəˋmɛntətɪv] (adj.) 好爭辯的
: often arguing or wanting to argue
- The discussion can never come to an end if you continue being so argumentative.
如果你繼續這樣爭論下去，討論永遠不可能有結果。

3.
* sarcastic [sɑrˋkæstɪk] (adj.) 諷刺的
: using sarcasm
- He used a sarcastic tone to raise the question to the manager.
他用諷刺的口吻向經理提出了這個問題。
