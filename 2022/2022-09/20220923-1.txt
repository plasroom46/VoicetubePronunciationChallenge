Pronunciation tips:

1. exhausted: (1) “x” is originally /ks/, which is voiceless. While here is the voiced sound. Therefore is /ɡz/. /ɪɡˈzɔː.stɪd/ (2) “au” is /ɔ/
2. sympathetic: (1)Four parts: sym-pa-the-tic /ˌsɪm.pəˈθet̬.ɪk/ (2) “t” is a flap t.
3. irritate (1)Three parts: i-rri-tate /ˈɪr.ə.teɪt/ (2) a_e is usually pronounced /eɪ/

1.
* sympathetic [͵sɪmpəˋθɛtɪk] (adj.) 有同情心的
: used to describe someone who shows, especially by what they say, that they understand and care about someone else's suffering
- People suffering from the war hope their government will lend a sympathetic ear to them and stop the war.
飽受戰爭之苦的人們希望他們的政府能理解同情他們，並停止戰爭。

2.
* irritate [ˋɪrə͵tet] (v.) 激怒
: to make someone angry or annoyed
- The reporter threw a sensitive question to the presidential candidate, trying to irritate her.
記者向總統候選人拋出了一個敏感問題，試圖激怒她。

3.
* daunt [dɔnt] (v.) 使氣餒
: to make someone feel slightly frightened or worried about their ability to achieve something
- She was daunted by the hard challenge and chose to drop out of the game.
她被這一艱難的挑戰嚇到了，選擇了退賽。
