Pronunciation tips:

1. asked: (1) The “ed” is pronounced /t/ here (2) Reason: “ed” is behind the voiceless sound /k/ (3) More voiceless example: /p/ helped, /x/ fixed.
2. underlying: (1) Two parts: under-lying (2) lying: pronunciation of /aɪ/
3. manipulation: (1) Five parts: ma-ni-pu-la-tion /məˌnɪp.jəˈleɪ.ʃən/ (2) /j/ sound in the third part: “pu”

1.
* underlying [͵ʌndɚˋlaɪɪŋ] (adj.) 潛在的
: real but not immediately obvious
- If you notice some details in the movie, you’ll figure out some underlying message that the director wants to convey.
如果你注意到電影中的一些細節，你會發現導演想要傳達的一些潛在信息。

2.
* unwitting [ʌnˋwɪtɪŋ] (adj.) 無意的
: without knowing or planning
- The drug addict claims that he is just the unwitting victim.
那名吸毒者聲稱，他只是一名不知情的受害者。

3.
* manipulation [mə͵nɪpjʊˋleʃən] (n.) 操縱
: controlling someone or something to your own advantage, often unfairly or dishonestly
- Some helicopter parents assert that they are protecting their children out of love, but that’s merely an excuse for manipulation.
一些直升機父母聲稱他們是出於愛而保護他們的孩子，但那不過是操縱的藉口罷了。
