2.
* uninhabited [ˌʌnɪnˈhæbɪtɪd] (adj.) 無人的 ； 無人居住的
: To inhabit means to live in a place. So uninhabited simply means that no one is living there.
- An island that is inhabited can also be called a desert island.
無人居住的島嶼又稱無人島。

3.
* settlement [ˈsɛtlmənt] (n.) 聚落；新拓居地
: a place where people come to live or the process of settling in such a place
- Many Native Americans were killed during the settlement of the American West by Europeans in the 19th century.
許多美洲印第安人在19世紀歐洲人在美國西部開拓殖民地的過程中被殺害。
