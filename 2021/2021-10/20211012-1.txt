Pronunciation tips:



1.
* threaten [ˈθrɛtn] (v.) 威脅；恐嚇
: to tell someone that you will kill or hurt them or cause problems if they do not do what you want
- The robbers threatened the shopkeeper with a gun.

2.
* trigger [ˈtrɪɡɚ] (v.) 觸發
: to cause something to start
- Some people find that certain foods trigger their headaches.
* trigger [ˈtrɪɡɚ] (n.) 板機 (槍)
: the part of a gun where you press to fire
- If you pull the trigger, you won’t know where the treasure is hidden.

3.
* effort [ˈɛfət] (n.) 努力；付出；工夫
: physical or mental activity needed to achieve something
- It takes a long time to prepare the dish but the results are so good that it's worth the effort.
