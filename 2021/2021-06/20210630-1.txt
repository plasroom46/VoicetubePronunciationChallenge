But memory extinction is not actually about old memories disappearing, it's about having both the old and new memories and your brain being able to phase the old one out as it becomes less relevant.

「記憶消除」並不是抹去舊的記憶，而是當你的腦中同時有新舊的記憶，大腦會淘汰舊的記憶，因為它比較不重要。

1.
* memory [ˋmɛmərɪ] (n.) 記憶力，記性
: the ability to remember information, experiences, and people
- What is your name again? I have a terrible memory.
可以再告訴我一次你的名字嗎？我的記憶力很糟糕。
* memorable [ˋmɛmərəb] (adj.) 難忘的；值得紀念的
: likely to be remembered or worth remembering
- Meeting the mayor and receiving an award from him was one of my most memorable moments in life.
見到市長並領他頒發的獎項是我人生中最難忘的事情之一。

2.
* extinction [ɪkˋstɪŋkʃən] (n.) 滅絕；絕種
: a situation in which something no longer exists
- When do you think humans will face extinction?
你覺得人類何時會面臨絕種？
* extinct [ɪkˋstɪŋkt] (adj.) 滅絕的；絕種的；消失的
: not now existing
- No, you can’t have a pet dinosaur, honey. Dinosaurs went extinct 65 million years ago.
親愛的，你不能養恐龍當寵物，恐龍 6500 萬年前就絕種了。

3.
* relevant [ˋrɛləvənt] (adj.) 有關的；相關聯的；切題的
: connected with what is happening or being discussed
- I have some information relevant to your research. Do you want to hear it?
我有一些與你的研究相關的資訊，你要聽嗎？
* irrelevant [ɪˋrɛləvənt] (adj.) 不相關的，不切題的
: not related to what is being discussed or considered and therefore not important
- Your point is irrelevant to this discussion.
你說的這點和這個討論主題毫無相關。
