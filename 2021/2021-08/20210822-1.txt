When exiting a conversation, interrupt yourself, not them. Make it clear that you enjoyed the conversation, and that you have a reason for leaving.

當你想退出談話時，打斷自己而不是別人。向大家表明你聊得很愉快，但有事得先走。

Pronunciation tips:

Blended words: that you (sounds like tha-choo)
Exiting - (instead of x sounding like ‘ks’, it sounds more like ‘gz’ so it sounds like egg-zitting)
Reason - long e, schwa sound

1.
* exit [ˋɛksɪt] (v.) 退出，離開
: V. 離開 to leave
- Please use the rear doors to exit the bus.
- If you hear the fire alarm, you must exit the building immediately.
: N.出口，安全門 the door through which you might leave a building or large vehicle, or the act of leaving something, especially a theatre stage
- Guting station has nine exits. 
- The restaurant is not far from Exit 2.

2.
* interrupt [͵ɪntəˋrʌpt] (v.) 打斷
: to stop a person from speaking for a short period by something you say or do
- The professor’s lecture was interrupted when a student’s phone started ringing.

3.
* clear [klɪr] (adj.) 清楚，明白
: easy to understand, hear, read, or see
- Harold is a new employee who’s just getting started, so make sure he has clear instructions on what he needs to do.
- When I take my glasses off, everything looks blurry. But when I put them back on, everything’s clear again.
