1. 
a. recipient (n.)  接受者；(電子信箱的)收信者；得獎人
  -> Oprah Winfrey was the recipient of Cecil B. DeMille award of 2018 Golden Globes.
    歐普拉是2018年金球獎的塞西爾戴米爾獎的得獎人。
b. receive (v.)  收到、接收 (信、包裹、讚美)
b. reception (n.)  接待、接見
  -> reception desk 服務台
  -> reception room 會客室
  -> You need to sign in before you visit at the reception desk over there.
    你在拜訪之前，你要在那邊的服務台登記簽名。
c. receptive (adj.)  樂於接受的
  -> Try to be receptive to other's experiences, you'll learn a lot from them.
    你要多聽、多接受他人的經驗，這樣你就可以學到很多。
  -> Gray is not a very receptive person. He always fight back against other's opinions if they conflict with his.
    Gray不是一個善於接受他人意見的人，他會反駁跟他不一樣的意見。
d. receipt (n.)  收據；發票
  * p 不發音
e. recipe (n.)  食譜、處方、秘訣
  -> It's an Italian recipe.
    它是來自義大利的料理。(這是義大利食譜。)
  -> My mom gave me the recipe.
    我媽教我食譜。
  -> He's going to give us the presentation about the recipe for success.
    他今天要分享他成功的秘訣。
2. 
a. acknowledgement (n.)  承認、確認、答謝
  * 一本書的一開始有時會有，感謝... 幫助完成這本書
  -> a letter of acknowledgement 感謝信
  -> Please take this small amount of money in acknowledgement of your help.
    這是感謝你幫忙的一點小心意，請你收下。
b. acknowledge (v.)  承認、表示熱忱
c. acknowledged (adj.)  公認的
  -> Mayday is generally acknowledged as one of the most popular band in Taiwan.
    五月天普遍被公認為台灣最有名氣的樂團之一。
3. 
a. recognition (n.)  認出、識別
  -> speech recognition 語音辨識
  -> beyond recognition 無法辨別
  -> speech interpretation and recognition interface (Siri)
b. recognize (v.)  認出、識別
  -> I can recognize my best friend's handwriting immediately.
    我可以馬上認出好朋友的筆跡。
c. recognizable (adj.)  可辨認的、可識別的
  -> After all these years, she is barely recognizable.
    經過這麼多年，我幾乎認不出她了。
4. 
a. satisfaction (n.)  滿意
b. satisfied (adj.)  滿意的
  * 主詞通常是人
  -> Poor Annie. Her mom is never satisfied with her grades, and forcing her to get a higher ranking in school.
    可憐的安妮，她媽媽永遠都不會滿意她的成績，並且逼她拿到更高的校排名。
c. satisfying (adj.)  滿意的
  * 通常指某件事情讓某人很滿足
d. contentment (n.)  滿足
  -> The little boy is holding his brand-new robot with a smile of contentment.
    小男孩心滿意足地笑著，手上抱著他的機器人。
e. fulfillment (n.)  滿足(感)、成就(感)
--------------------------------------
Though they do not expect the recipient to be grateful or thankful to them, they seek acknowledgement from those close to them. Introverts help others not to get recognition for their good works, but because it gives them a sense of satisfaction.
