1. 
a. constraint (n.)  限制
  -> We had to adjust our plans for the event due to budget constraint.
    由於預算限制，我們需要調整我們的對這件事的計畫。
b. restriction (n.)  限制
  * 法律、規範面的限制
  -> During the summer, the government put restrictions on water use.
    夏季，政府頒布了限水的規範。
c. restraint (n.)  阻止；克制
  * 常用來指對情緒或思想的控制
  -> Ben showed a lot of restraint when dealing with the rude customer.
    Ben在跟這個無理的客人周旋時，展現了很強的自制力。
2. 
a. attract (v.)  吸引
  * 讓人喜歡、對某事感興趣，或向某地靠近
  -> The Eiffel Tower attracts tourists from around the world.
    艾菲爾鐵塔吸引了世界各地的觀光客前來。
  -> 人1 is attracted to 人2
  -> Jerry is attracted to his coworker Emma.
    Jerry被他的同事Emma所吸引。
b. attractive (adj.)  有吸引力的
  -> The job offer is very attractive.
    這個職缺很吸引人。
  -> Kevin is a nice guy, but he’s not very attractive.
    Kevin 是個好人，但他長得不怎麼樣。(ＱＱ)
c. attraction (n.)  吸引；吸引力
  -> Time square is a popular tourist attraction.
    時代廣場是一個熱門觀光景點。
  -> As soon as Kate met Tyler, she felt an attraction to him.
    當Kate遇到Tyler，她覺得他有一種吸引力。
3. 
a. psychological (adj.)  心理的；心理學的
  -> Depression is a common psychological disorder.
    憂鬱症是一個常見的心理疾病。
b. psychology (n.)  心理學；心理
  -> He has a degree in psychology.
    他有心理學學位。
  -> The investigators want to understand the criminal psychology.
    調查員們想了解這個罪犯的心理。
c. psychologist (n.)  心理學家
  -> If you are struggling with your mental health, you might want to see a psychologist.
    如果你為心理疾病所苦，你可能會想看心理醫生。
4. 
a. predispose (v.)  使預先有傾向（或意向）
  -> Obesity predisposes you to other health issues, such as heart disease.
    肥胖讓你的健康的有易出問題的傾向，比如說心臟病。
b. dispose (v.)  使傾向於
  -> Jerry’s personality disposes him to conflict with others.
    Jerry的個性讓他容易跟別人產生衝突。
5. 
a. fall for (phr.)  迷戀,愛上；信以為真
  -> Linda fell for her classmate, Tony.
    Linda愛上了她的同學Tony。
  -> I felt stupid for falling for my little brother’s trick.
    我覺得我相信我弟弟的惡作劇實在是太蠢了。
b. head over heels (phr.)  完全地
  * 幾乎都用在愛情方面
  -> Linda is head over heels for Tony.
    Linda完全愛上了Tony。
--------------------------------------
Some very real constraints around whom we can love and feel properly attracted to come from a place we might not think to look: our childhoods. Our psychological history strongly predisposes us to fall for only certain types of people.
