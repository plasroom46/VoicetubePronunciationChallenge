1. 
a. counterproductive (adj.)  產生不良後果的
  -> Yelling at Eric was counterproductive, as it only made him more angry.
    對Eric叫喊會產生反效果，它只會讓他更生氣。
b. counter (v.)  反對；反擊
  -> The army plans to counter the attack by their enemy.
    這個軍隊計劃對敵人的攻勢發動反擊。
c. counterargument (n.)  用來反駁的論點
  -> Our team won the debate, thanks to our strong counterargument.
    多虧我們強力的論點，我們的隊伍贏了這場辯論。
2. 
a. species (n.)  種類
  * 一群相似的生物
  -> There are over four thousand species of frogs.
    青蛙的種類有四千多種。
b. breed (n.)  品種
  * 通常指飼養的動物或植物
  -> My favorite dog breeds are Chihuahua and French Bulldog.
    我最喜歡的狗是吉娃娃跟法國鬥牛犬。
3. 
a. cannibalism (n.)  自相殘殺
b. cannibal (n.)  同類相食的動物；食人者
  -> Praying mantises are cannibals.
    螳螂是會同類相食的動物。
4. 
a. cull (v.)  撲殺
  -> Dairy farmers often cull cows that cannot produce milk.
    奶農通常會撲殺無法產牛奶的母牛。
b. eliminate (v.)  消滅
  -> eliminate the enemy 消滅敵人
  -> The government is working to eliminate this disease.
    政府正致力於消滅這種疾病。
5. 
a. bolster (v.)  支撐; 增強
  -> bolster the economy 拼經濟
  -> The teacher’s praise bolstered the students’ confidence.
    老師的鼓勵增強了學生的自信心。
b. boost (v.)  提高；增加
  -> The new machine boosted production.
    這台新機器提高了生產力。
c. reinforce (v.)  增援
  -> The speech reinforced my beliefs.
    這場演講增強了我的信仰。
-------------------------------------- 
While it may seem counterproductive for members of the same species to eat each other, cannibalism can promote the survival of the species as a whole by reducing competition, culling the weak, and bolstering the strong.
