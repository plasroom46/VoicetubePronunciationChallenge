1. 
a. container (n.) 貨櫃(海運or空運等)、容器
b. contain (v.) 包含、容納、控制
                (這個東西的主要構成要素)

c. include (v.) 包括、包含
                (這個東西是由小部分所組成)
  -> The rent includes the furniture 
      and utility bills.
      這個租金包含了傢俱和水電瓦斯費。
  -> water bill 水費
  -> electricity bill 電費
  -> gas bill 瓦斯費
2. 
a. municipal (a.) 市立的、自治的、內政的
                    (正式用法)
b. urban 
  <-> rural 鄉村的
  -> People are increasingly moving away 
     from small towns to live in urban areas.
    很多人從小城鎮搬到市中心去居住。

c. civic (a.) 城市的、公民的
  -> civic center 市中心
3. 
a. discard (v.) 拋棄、丟棄
                (n.) 被拋棄的東西or人
  -> 動機：覺得東西沒用所以丟棄
  -> Some chemical plants discard waste 
      products into rivers for their convenience, 
      leading to severe water contamination.
     有些化工廠把廢棄物排入河川，造成
     嚴重的水污染。
  -> pollution 污染 (整體環境)
  -> contamination 污染 (由某個特定物質所造成)

b. abandon (v.) 拋棄、遺棄、放棄
  -> 動機：刻意遺棄或放棄東西
4. 
a. mindless (a.) 沒頭腦的、愚蠢的、不小心的
  -> Eating in front of a television fosters 
      mindless munching.
    邊看電視邊吃飯會讓你沒辦法認真咀嚼食物。
  -> foster 促進
  -> munch 咀嚼

b. mindful (a.) 留心的、小心的
5. 
a. consumption (n.) 消耗、消費
b. consume (v.) 消耗、花費
  -> Compared with developing countries, 
      developed countries consume more energy.
     跟開發中國家比起來，已開發國家消耗
     更多能源。
c. consumer (n.) 消費者

These little containers and wrappers may not seem like a big deal, but in the U.S., packaging makes up the largest category of municipal waste. On top of that, single-use items make up another 10 percent of all our discards. And this kind of mindless consumption has a really big impact on climate change.
